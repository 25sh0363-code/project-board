1. Overview
Cancer major health burden in Russia with geographic disparities in care access.

2. Context
Cancer care evolved significantly. Challenges remain in early detection, treatment access in remote regions, and lifestyle risk factors.

3. Common Types
Lung, breast, colorectal, stomach, prostate cancers common. High tobacco-related cancers.

4. Risk Factors
High tobacco and alcohol use, diet, occupational exposures, environmental factors, aging population

5. Diagnosis/Treatment
Expanding cancer centers, improving diagnostics. Urban-rural disparities significant.

6. Trends
High incidence
Improving detection and treatment
Geographic disparities
Late diagnosis common

7. Challenges
Late presentation
Geographic access disparities
Tobacco/alcohol control
Healthcare infrastructure in remote areas

8. Prevention
Tobacco control, alcohol reduction, healthy lifestyle, screening programs

9. Public Health
Federal cancer programs, expanding oncology services, cancer registries, research

10. Summary
Russia addresses cancer through federal programs, healthcare infrastructure development, and expanding access across vast territory, while managing challenges of risk factors and disparities.
