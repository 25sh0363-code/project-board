1. Overview
HIV attacks immune system. AIDS is advanced untreated HIV. Canada has achieved good HIV control but challenges remain.

2. Historical Context
Epidemic emerged 1980s. Strong community response. Universal healthcare enables treatment access. Harm reduction pioneered.

3. Transmission
Sexual contact, needle sharing, mother-to-child

4. Clinical Course
Acute, chronic, AIDS if untreated. ART prevents progression.

5. Diagnosis
Testing available nationwide including rapid tests

6. Treatment
ART universally accessible through healthcare. PrEP available. U=U recognized.

7. Current Trends
Steady new infections
High diagnosis and treatment rates
Disparities: Indigenous peoples, MSM, people who inject drugs
Mother-to-child transmission rare

8. Projections
Goal to end HIV epidemic
Long-acting treatments
Vaccine/cure research

9. Challenges
Indigenous health disparities
Stigma
Reaching marginalized populations
Provincial variations in PrEP access

10. Prevention
Condoms, PrEP, needle exchange, supervised consumption sites, testing, education, treatment as prevention

11. Public Health
Federal HIV/AIDS Initiative, provincial programs, community organizations, universal healthcare, harm reduction, research

12. Summary
Canada addresses HIV through universal healthcare, harm reduction leadership, community partnerships, and comprehensive prevention-treatment approach while working to eliminate disparities.
