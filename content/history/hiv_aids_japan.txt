1. Overview
HIV attacks immune system. Japan has low HIV prevalence but challenges in diagnosis and awareness.

2. Historical Context
Early cases in 1980s included contaminated blood products (hemophiliacs). Current epidemic mainly in MSM. Diagnosis often late.

3. Transmission
Sexual contact (MSM primary), needle sharing (rare), blood products (historical)

4. Diagnosis/Treatment
ART available through healthcare. Testing not widespread. Late diagnosis common.

5. Current Trends
Steady new infections
Cases mainly in MSM, particularly Tokyo/Osaka
Late diagnosis common (many present with AIDS)
Low testing rates
Stigma significant

6. Projections
Increasing awareness efforts
Expanding testing
Earlier diagnosis goals

7. Challenges
Low testing rates
Late diagnosis
Stigma and discrimination
Limited public awareness
Cultural barriers to discussion

8. Prevention
Condom promotion, education (limited), testing expansion needed, PrEP becoming available

9. Public Health
National HIV/AIDS strategy, healthcare coverage, research, increasing awareness efforts

10. Summary
Japan faces HIV challenges despite low prevalence due to late diagnosis, limited testing, stigma, and cultural barriers requiring expanded awareness, testing, and early treatment initiatives.
