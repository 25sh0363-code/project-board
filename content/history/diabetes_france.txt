1. Conceptual Overview
Diabetes mellitus is a metabolic disorder with chronic hyperglycemia causing long-term organ damage.

France has comprehensive diabetes care through its universal healthcare system, though prevalence continues to rise.

2. Historical Evolution
Diabetes management has advanced significantly in France. Prevalence has increased since the 1980s due to aging, obesity, and lifestyle changes. France's healthcare system provides universal access to diabetes care and medications.

3. Pathophysiology
Type 2 Diabetes (most common): insulin resistance and beta-cell failure
Type 1 Diabetes: autoimmune destruction
Gestational Diabetes

4. Clinical Manifestations
Progressive symptoms from early fatigue to established polyuria/polydipsia to advanced complications

5. Non-Communicable Nature
Risk factors: genetics, obesity, physical inactivity, age, diet

6. Treatment Architecture
Lifestyle modification, medications (metformin, others), insulin, comprehensive monitoring, multidisciplinary care
