1. Overview
Cancer leading cause of death. South Korea has advanced cancer care with comprehensive screening and treatment systems.

2. Context
Rapid development of cancer care infrastructure. National Cancer Screening Program provides extensive coverage.

3. Common Types
Gastric, lung, colorectal, breast, liver cancers most common.

4. Risk Factors
Aging population, tobacco, alcohol, diet (high salt), H. pylori, Hepatitis B, obesity

5. Diagnosis/Treatment
Advanced cancer centers, comprehensive screening programs, cutting-edge treatments, high technology adoption

6. Trends
High incidence
Excellent survival rates (among world's best for many cancers)
Comprehensive screening culture
Advanced treatment access

7. Challenges
Aging population
Lifestyle-related cancers increasing
Treatment costs despite insurance

8. Prevention
Tobacco control, healthy lifestyle, Hepatitis B vaccination, H. pylori treatment, comprehensive screening (stomach, breast, cervical, colorectal, liver, lung)

9. Public Health
National Cancer Center, National Cancer Screening Program, universal health insurance, research investment

10. Summary
South Korea addresses cancer through universal healthcare, comprehensive national screening program, advanced treatment centers, and strong research achieving world-leading survival rates.
