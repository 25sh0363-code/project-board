1. Overview
TB infectious disease. Japan has low TB incidence among developed nations but higher than some Western countries.

2. History
Major historical health problem. Post-war TB control successful. Current cases mainly in elderly and vulnerable populations.

3. Pathophysiology
Mycobacterial infection, latent vs active

4. Manifestations
Pulmonary TB primary form

5. Transmission
Airborne droplets

6. Diagnosis
Advanced diagnostic systems

7. Treatment
Standard multi-drug therapy, strong treatment support systems

8. Trends
Declining incidence
Cases concentrated in elderly (aging population effect)
Low drug resistance
Comprehensive control program

9. Projections
Continued decline
Aging population challenges

10. Challenges
Elderly population TB
Treatment in aging patients
Urban TB transmission

11. Prevention
Screening, BCG vaccination, contact investigation, latent TB treatment

12. Public Health
National TB program, mandatory notification, research, universal healthcare access

13. Summary
Japan maintains TB control through comprehensive program, universal healthcare, and strong public health infrastructure, with particular focus on elderly populations.
