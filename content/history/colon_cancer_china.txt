1. Overview
Cancer involves uncontrolled cell growth. China has world's largest cancer burden by absolute numbers.

2. Context
Cancer prevalence increased with industrialization, aging, lifestyle changes. Major public health challenge.

3. Common Types
Lung, gastric, liver, esophageal, colorectal cancers most common. Patterns differ from Western countries.

4. Risk Factors
Tobacco (especially in men), air pollution, occupational exposures, infections (HBV, HCV, H. pylori), dietary factors

5. Diagnosis/Treatment
Expanding diagnostic and treatment capacity. Urban-rural disparities significant.

6. Trends
Rising incidence
Improving survival with better treatment access
Late diagnosis common
Urban-rural disparities

7. Challenges
Massive scale
Late presentation
Geographic disparities
High costs
Insufficient oncology workforce

8. Prevention
Tobacco control, Hepatitis B vaccination, screening programs, air quality improvement

9. Public Health
National cancer programs, expanding insurance coverage, research investment, cancer registries

10. Summary
China addresses massive cancer burden through healthcare system expansion, prevention programs, and research, while managing challenges of scale and disparities.
