1. Overview
HIV attacks immune system. France has controlled HIV epidemic through comprehensive healthcare and prevention.

2. Historical Context
Early epidemic impacted gay men, people who inject drugs. Strong public health response. Universal healthcare enables treatment access.

3. Transmission
Sexual contact, needle sharing

4. Diagnosis/Treatment
Comprehensive testing, ART universally accessible, PrEP available, U=U recognized

5. Current Trends
Declining new infections
High treatment coverage
Cases in MSM, migrants from endemic regions
Mother-to-child transmission eliminated

6. Projections
Continued decline
Long-acting treatments
Elimination goals

7. Challenges
Reaching migrants
Stigma
Late diagnosis in some groups

8. Prevention
Condoms, PrEP, needle exchange, testing, education, treatment as prevention

9. Public Health
National HIV strategy, universal healthcare, comprehensive prevention, research

10. Summary
France controls HIV through universal healthcare, comprehensive prevention-treatment, strong public health programs achieving declining incidence and virtual elimination of mother-to-child transmission.
