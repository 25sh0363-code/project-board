1. Overview
TB infectious disease. Russia faces significant TB burden including high rates of drug-resistant TB.

2. History
TB increased dramatically during Soviet collapse. Modern efforts focus on control and addressing drug resistance.

3. Pathophysiology
Mycobacterial infection with high rates of drug resistance

4. Manifestations
Pulmonary TB primary, extrapulmonary cases

5. Transmission
Airborne, increased risk in prisons, healthcare settings, crowded conditions

6. Diagnosis
Diagnostic systems including drug susceptibility testing

7. Treatment
Standard regimens, extensive drug-resistant TB treatment programs

8. Trends
High TB incidence
Significant multi-drug resistant TB
Decreasing trends with sustained efforts
Prison TB major concern

9. Projections
Continued decline with sustained programs
Drug resistance challenges

10. Challenges
Drug-resistant TB
Prison TB
Treatment adherence
Geographic disparities
Alcoholism, HIV co-infection

11. Prevention
Screening, DOT, infection control, prison TB programs

12. Public Health
Federal TB program, international support, research, drug resistance surveillance

13. Summary
Russia addresses significant TB burden including world's highest drug-resistant TB rates through federal programs, international partnerships, and focused efforts on high-risk populations.
