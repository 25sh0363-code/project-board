1. Overview
TB infectious disease. Germany has low TB incidence with cases mainly in foreign-born populations.

2. History
Historically major health problem. Modern era saw dramatic decline. Current cases concentrated in migrants and vulnerable groups.

3. Pathophysiology
Bacterial infection, latent vs active, drug resistance

4. Manifestations
Primarily pulmonary TB

5. Transmission
Airborne

6. Diagnosis
Comprehensive diagnostic capabilities

7. Treatment
Standard multi-drug regimens, strong adherence support

8. Trends
Low incidence
Cases in foreign-born (majority)
Drug resistance monitoring
Robust control system

9. Projections
Maintaining low rates
Continued focus on migrant health

10. Challenges
Integrating migrant populations into care
Language barriers
Treatment completion

11. Prevention
Screening, contact investigation, latent TB treatment

12. Public Health
Robert Koch Institute coordination, mandatory reporting, universal healthcare access, research

13. Summary
Germany maintains low TB through comprehensive healthcare system, strong public health infrastructure, and targeted programs for migrant populations.
