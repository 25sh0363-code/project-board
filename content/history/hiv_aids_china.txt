1. Overview
HIV attacks immune system; AIDS is advanced stage. China has significant HIV burden with evolving epidemic patterns.

2. Historical Context
Early epidemic driven by blood plasma donation practices. Later sexual transmission became primary route. Government response strengthened over time.

3. Transmission
Sexual contact (now primary), needle sharing, historical blood transfusion/plasma donation

4. Clinical Progression
Acute, chronic, AIDS without treatment

5. Diagnosis/Treatment
Expanding testing and treatment access. Free ART through government programs. Treatment coverage improving.

6. Current Trends
Steady new infections
Sexual transmission predominant
Increasing cases among MSM, youth
Treatment coverage improving
Stigma remains significant

7. Projections
Expanding prevention and treatment
Reducing stigma
Targeted interventions

8. Challenges
Stigma and discrimination
Reaching key populations
Treatment adherence
Rural access

9. Prevention
Education, condoms, needle exchange (limited), testing expansion, treatment as prevention

10. Public Health
National HIV/AIDS programs, free treatment policy, expanding services, research

11. Summary
China addresses HIV through government programs, free treatment, expanding services while managing challenges of stigma, key population reach, and epidemic evolution.
