1. Overview
HIV attacks immune system. South Korea has low but steadily increasing HIV cases.

2. Historical Context
First case 1985. Long low prevalence. Gradual increase in recent years, primarily among MSM.

3. Transmission
Sexual contact (MSM primary route), heterosexual contact

4. Diagnosis/Treatment
ART available through National Health Insurance. Free testing available. Mandatory testing for some groups (military, foreigners for certain visas).

5. Current Trends
Steady increase in new diagnoses
Cases mainly in MSM
Increasing among young people
High late diagnosis rate
Low public awareness
Significant stigma

6. Projections
Increasing awareness
Expanding prevention
Earlier diagnosis
PrEP availability expanding

7. Challenges
Stigma and discrimination
Low public awareness
Late diagnosis
Mandatory disclosure policies
Cultural barriers
Limited LGBT healthcare services

8. Prevention
Condom promotion, education (expanding), testing, PrEP (recently available), treatment as prevention

9. Public Health
Korea Disease Control and Prevention Agency, National Health Insurance coverage, HIV/AIDS Prevention Act, research, increasing awareness efforts

10. Summary
South Korea addresses increasing HIV cases through healthcare coverage, expanding prevention including PrEP, and awareness efforts while managing challenges of stigma, late diagnosis, and cultural barriers.
