1. Overview
HIV attacks immune system; AIDS is advanced stage. India has third-largest HIV burden globally but prevalence declining.

2. Historical Context
Epidemic recognized 1980s. Initially spread through sex workers, truck drivers, injecting drug users. Strong National AIDS Control Programme launched. Epidemic stabilized and declining.

3. Transmission
Heterosexual contact (primary), needle sharing, mother-to-child, blood transfusion (now rare)

4. Diagnosis/Treatment
Free testing and ART through government programs. Treatment coverage expanded significantly. Prevention of mother-to-child transmission programs.

5. Current Trends
Declining new infections and AIDS deaths
Regional variations (high in some states)
Key populations: sex workers, MSM, people who inject drugs, truckers
Increasing treatment coverage
Mother-to-child transmission reduced

6. Projections
Continued decline
Elimination goals
Expanding services

7. Challenges
Stigma and discrimination
Reaching marginalized populations
Rural access
Treatment adherence
Linkage to care

8. Prevention
Condom promotion, needle exchange (limited), testing, education, treatment as prevention, targeted interventions for key populations

9. Public Health
National AIDS Control Programme, free testing and treatment, targeted interventions, community mobilization, research

10. Summary
India achieved remarkable HIV response success through National AIDS Control Programme providing free testing-treatment, targeted interventions, community mobilization resulting in declining epidemic while addressing remaining challenges of stigma and access.
