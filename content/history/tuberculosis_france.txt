1. Overview
TB is infectious bacterial disease. France has low TB incidence compared to global average, with cases concentrated in vulnerable populations.

2. History
TB major historical health problem. Modern antibiotic era reduced TB dramatically. Current cases mainly in foreign-born, socioeconomically disadvantaged, and high-risk groups.

3. Pathophysiology
Mycobacterial infection, latent vs active, drug resistance

4. Manifestations
Pulmonary and extrapulmonary presentations

5. Transmission
Airborne droplets

6. Diagnosis
Standard TB diagnostics including molecular tests

7. Treatment
Multi-drug regimens, directly observed therapy for high-risk cases

8. Trends
Low but stable incidence
Cases in foreign-born persons
Some drug resistance
Strong control program

9. Projections
Maintaining low incidence
Focus on vulnerable populations

10. Challenges
Reaching marginalized populations
Homelessness, migration
Treatment adherence

11. Prevention
Screening, contact tracing, latent TB treatment, infection control

12. Public Health
National TB program, universal healthcare access, surveillance, public health infrastructure

13. Summary
France maintains low TB rates through comprehensive public health programs, universal healthcare, and targeted interventions for vulnerable populations.
