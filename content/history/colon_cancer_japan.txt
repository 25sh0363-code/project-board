1. Overview
Cancer leading cause of death in Japan. Comprehensive healthcare system provides universal cancer care access.

2. Context
Aging population drives cancer burden. Strong prevention, screening, and treatment infrastructure.

3. Common Types
Gastric, lung, colorectal, breast, prostate cancers most common.

4. Risk Factors
Aging, tobacco, alcohol, salt intake, H. pylori infection, obesity, physical inactivity

5. Diagnosis/Treatment
Advanced diagnostics, comprehensive cancer centers, multidisciplinary care, high technology adoption

6. Trends
High incidence due to aging
Improving survival rates
Strong screening culture (annual health checks)
Declining gastric cancer (H. pylori treatment)

7. Challenges
Aging population burden
Cancer care workforce needs
Treatment costs

8. Prevention
Tobacco control, healthy lifestyle, H. pylori eradication, vaccination (HPV, Hepatitis B), comprehensive screening

9. Public Health
National Cancer Center, mandatory annual health checks, cancer registries, research funding

10. Summary
Japan addresses cancer through universal healthcare, comprehensive screening systems, advanced treatment, and strong research within context of rapidly aging population.
