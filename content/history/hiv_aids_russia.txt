1. Overview
HIV attacks immune system. Russia has significant HIV epidemic, particularly among people who inject drugs.

2. Historical Context
Epidemic exploded in late 1990s/early 2000s driven by injecting drug use. Later sexual transmission increased. Response hampered by stigma, limited harm reduction.

3. Transmission
Needle sharing (historically primary), heterosexual contact (increasing), mother-to-child

4. Diagnosis/Treatment
ART availability expanded but coverage gaps remain. Testing available but stigma limits uptake.

5. Current Trends
High HIV prevalence
Shift from drug use to sexual transmission
Increasing heterosexual transmission
Significant AIDS mortality
Treatment coverage improving but gaps remain

6. Projections
Expanding treatment access
Addressing harm reduction
Reducing stigma

7. Challenges
Limited harm reduction (needle exchange, opioid substitution)
Stigma and discrimination
Treatment access and adherence
HIV-TB co-infection
Reaching key populations

8. Prevention
Condoms, education, testing, treatment as prevention. Harm reduction limited.

9. Public Health
Federal HIV programs, expanding ART access, international support, research

10. Summary
Russia addresses significant HIV epidemic transitioning from injecting drug use to sexual transmission, requiring expanded treatment, comprehensive harm reduction, and stigma reduction.
