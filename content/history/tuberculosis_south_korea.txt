1. Overview
TB infectious disease. South Korea has highest TB burden among OECD countries but declining.

2. History
Historically widespread. Post-war development reduced TB but rates remained elevated. Recent decades show declining trends with intensive control efforts.

3. Pathophysiology
Mycobacterial infection, latent vs active

4. Manifestations
Pulmonary TB predominant

5. Transmission
Airborne transmission

6. Diagnosis
Advanced diagnostic infrastructure

7. Treatment
Standard regimens, public-private mix DOTS, free treatment

8. Trends
Declining incidence
Still highest among OECD
Cases in elderly, vulnerable populations
Some drug resistance

9. Projections
Continued decline toward OECD average
Technology integration

10. Challenges
Elderly population TB
Workplace transmission
Stigma
Treatment adherence

11. Prevention
Screening programs, contact investigation, latent TB treatment, BCG vaccination

12. Public Health
National TB Elimination Plan, universal healthcare coverage, public-private collaboration, research

13. Summary
South Korea addresses TB through comprehensive national program, universal healthcare, public-private partnerships, and intensive control efforts aimed at reaching OECD average levels.
