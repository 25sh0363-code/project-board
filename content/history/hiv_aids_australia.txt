1. Overview
HIV attacks immune system; AIDS is advanced stage. Australia has been global leader in HIV response.

2. Historical Context
Early rapid community-government partnership response. Strong prevention programs. High treatment coverage achieved.

3. Transmission
Sexual contact, needle sharing, mother-to-child (rare with treatment)

4. Clinical Progression
Acute infection, chronic phase, AIDS if untreated

5. Diagnosis
HIV testing widely available, including rapid tests

6. Treatment
ART universally accessible through healthcare system. U=U principle. PrEP available.

7. Current Trends
Low new infection rates
High rates of diagnosis, treatment, viral suppression
Cases mainly in gay/bisexual men
Very low mother-to-child transmission

8. Projections
On track for virtual elimination of transmission
Long-acting treatments
Continued research

9. Challenges
Reaching remaining undiagnosed
Stigma reduction
Indigenous populations
Maintaining political/funding commitment

10. Prevention
Condoms, PrEP, needle exchange, testing, education, treatment as prevention

11. Public Health
National HIV Strategy, universal healthcare access, community partnerships, research

12. Summary
Australia's HIV response through community-government partnership, universal healthcare, comprehensive prevention, and high treatment coverage serves as global model approaching virtual elimination.
