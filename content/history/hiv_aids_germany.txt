1. Overview
HIV infects immune system. Germany has well-controlled HIV epidemic.

2. Historical Context
Epidemic emerged 1980s. Comprehensive response through healthcare system and prevention programs.

3. Transmission
Sexual contact (primary), needle sharing

4. Diagnosis/Treatment
ART universally accessible through healthcare, PrEP available, comprehensive testing

5. Current Trends
Stable new infections
High treatment coverage and viral suppression
Cases mainly in MSM
Mother-to-child transmission eliminated

6. Projections
Maintaining low rates
Long-acting treatments
Elimination strategies

7. Challenges
Reaching migrants, late diagnosis in some groups, maintaining prevention efforts

8. Prevention
Condoms, PrEP, needle programs, testing, education, treatment as prevention

9. Public Health
National HIV/AIDS Strategy, Robert Koch Institute, universal healthcare, comprehensive services

10. Summary
Germany controls HIV through universal healthcare, comprehensive prevention-treatment programs, strong surveillance achieving stable low incidence and elimination of mother-to-child transmission.
